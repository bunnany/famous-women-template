Dame Whina Cooper

Dame Whina Cooper was a distinguished Māori leader known for her lifelong advocacy for Māori rights. She is best remembered for leading the 1975 Māori Land March, a significant protest highlighting the struggle for Māori land and justice in New Zealand.

Dame Kiri Te Kanawa

An internationally renowned opera singer, Dame Kiri Te Kanawa has performed on some of the world's most prestigious stages. Her exceptional talent and contributions to music have made her one of New Zealand's most famous cultural figures.

Jacinda Ardern

Jacinda Ardern is a New Zealand politician who served as the 40th prime minister of New Zealand and leader of the Labour Party from 2017 to 2023. She is the youngest person to hold the office of prime minister since Edward Stafford in 1856, and the third woman to hold the office.

Malala Yousafzai

Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani education activist who became the youngest-ever Nobel Prize laureate. She has been an advocate for girls' education and women's rights, despite facing assassination attempts and threats from the Taliban.

Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace was an English mathematician and writer. She is best known for her work on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine. Lovelace is often regarded as the first computer programmer.

Rosa Parks

Rosa Parks was an African American civil rights activist known for her pivotal role in the Montgomery bus boycott. Her refusal to give up her seat to a white passenger sparked a major movement against racial segregation.
